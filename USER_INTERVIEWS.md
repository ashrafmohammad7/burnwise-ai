# User Interviews

## Interview 1

### Person
Saketh — Final-year Computer Science student building freelance web projects.

### Direct Quotes

- "I use ChatGPT and Cursor together almost daily."
- "Sometimes I forget which subscriptions are actually worth paying for."
- "I usually test multiple AI tools and forget to cancel older plans."

### Most Surprising Insight

The user mentioned they were paying for overlapping AI tools mainly because they wanted “backup options” rather than because they actively used all of them.

### What Changed In The Product

This conversation pushed me to add more realistic recommendations around duplicate tooling and overlapping subscriptions.

## Interview 2

### Person
Harsha — Freelance frontend developer working with small startup clients.

### Direct Quotes

- "GitHub Copilot is useful, but I don't always need the paid version."
- "A lot of small teams just copy what bigger startups use."
- "Most people never calculate annual AI costs."

### Most Surprising Insight

The user cared more about predictable monthly expenses than advanced AI features.

### What Changed In The Product

I improved the annual savings visibility and made the optimization report more financially focused instead of feature-focused.

## Interview 3

### Person
Karthikeya — Student developer experimenting with Claude, Gemini, and ChatGPT for research and coding.

### Direct Quotes

- "I switch tools depending on the task."
- "Sometimes Gemini and ChatGPT feel interchangeable."
- "If a tool saves only a few dollars, I probably wouldn't switch."

### Most Surprising Insight

The user only considered changing tools when savings felt significant enough to justify workflow changes.

### What Changed In The Product

This led me to make recommendations more conservative and avoid forcing optimization suggestions when savings were minimal.
